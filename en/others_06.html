Where are the Bus Lanes?

Dedicating bus lanes for the sole use of public and taxis ensure the timely arrival of buses at bus stops.

The bus lane project is an offshoot of the Fourth Strategic Goal of the RTA to encourage the use of mass public transport.

The initial phase of the dedicated bus Lane Project took effect from May 2010 over selected roads in high density areas and congested spots in Dubai. Phase II of the project was announced in Jan 2011 following a highly favorable reception for Phase I.

The initial phase of the Dedicated Bus & Taxi Lanes project created 5.6 km of dedicated lanes covering:

  • Al Mankhool Road (about 1400 meters from Al Satwa R/A up to Sheikh Rashid Road).
  • Al Khaleej Road (about 3660 meters from Khalid bin Al Waleed Road Intersection up to Al Musalla Road opposite the Hyatt Regency Hotel).
  • Khalid bin Al Waleed Road (about 220 meter from Al Mina Road Intersection up to Street 16).
  • Al Ghubaiba Road (about 320 meter from Al Mina Intersection up to Street 12).

Phase II of the dedicated Lanes added:

  • Dedicated lanes in parts of Naif Road, extending to 1 km.
  • 1 km of dedicated lanes at Al Ittihad Road.

Misuse of dedicated bus and taxi lanes can result in a fine of AED 600.
